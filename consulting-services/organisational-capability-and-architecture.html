Organisational Capability and Architecture


ConsultCambridge helps top teams to create a robust vision of their future capable organisation. Through structured facilitation, rigorous analysis and research derived frameworks, we establish concrete strategic and organisational priorities and identify current and future organisational capability requirements.

We further enable organization wide engagement and creative input to identify organisational design options and craft a fir for purpose organisational architecture for long term and sustainable value creation.


The focus of our integrated and customized services encompass:





  • Articulating strategy and organisation linkages: our focus is on addressing key strategic and organisational priorities and translating them into key inputs to the capability and architecture decision-making process. We combine industry insights with clinical case studies from peer organisations to best map the strategic and organisation needs in the short, medium and long term.


  • Visioning the future state organisation: we bring a robust analytical focus to assessing future state organisational requirement in respect of capabilities and resource investments. We work with top teams to understand in depth the product or service offering relative market opportunity and challenge and the implications for the organisational shape, structure and behaviour.


  • Formulating the fit for purpose organisational architecture: in line with the vision of the future state, we engage widely throughout the organisation to formulate a best fit for purpose organisational architecture through which long term sustainable value might be realised.


  • Prioritising strategic resource investments: We help clients prioritise resource investments in line with strategic and organisational priorities. Our approach is consider the variety of resources available and select those, human, social and organisational capitals, which yield the organisational capability necessary for strategic advantage.

Breaking With Tradition

Dr Jonathan Trevor

Cambridge Judge Business School


Dr Jonathan Trevor argues that we will see a move away from large scale public sector organisations which are the providers of professional services to public services organisations which are the commissioners of professionals from the public, private and not for profit sector to provide these services. The question he asks is how do we ensure these public servants and organisations are best fit for purpose to deliver this agenda.
