Human Capital Solutions



ConsultCambridge provides a range of innovative human capital solutions relating to human capital planning, management and innovations in aspects of talent management, workforce planning and employment relationships. We work with HR teams and business unit heads to address issues around talent selection, development, segmentation, succession, performance management and reward.

A key focus is the development of strategy aligned HR outcomes and systems that are embedded in a clear business framework. We further support HR teams to develop functional excellence and build capability for HR led transformation.




  • Human Capital Planning: we develop robust and explicit linkages between strategic capability requirements and human capital investments. Strategically valuable roles are segmented and skills, competencies, behaviours and contribution, modelled to match organisational priorities. The outcome is a robust human capital architecture, identification of critical roles and the development of compelling propositions to key talent segments.


  • Designing Best In Class HR Systems: we design multi-level HR systems emphasising vertical integration between HR practices and strategic outcomes, and horizontal integration between complementary HR processes. The result is a strong HR system, aligned to both strategic and employee need, and an end to end logic that encompasses the HR system approach, design and implementation.


  • HR Function Excellence: we work with HR teams to ground their role in a clear business need, enhance functional capabilities and maximise their impact as a leader of organisation wide transformation. Behavioural and technical expertise is developed through facilitation and leadership education to ensure value added contribution. We seek to transfer knowledge to promote best in class people, relationships, networks, culture and leadership practice.

Can Pay be Strategic?


Dr Jonathan Trevor

This book provides a timely insight into the challenges faced by leading organizations when attempting to deploy pay systems in the pursuit of strategic priorities. Based upon in-depth case study research, this book reveals a number of managerial obstacles to the effective management of contemporary pay systems and the dysfunctionalities and negative outcomes that are an organizational reality for many.


Difficult to get right and easy to get wrong, are attempts to use pay strategically worth it? To this on-going and critical debate in the field, Jonathan Trevor offers for both academics and practitioners a fresh perspective grounded in robust theory and relevant empirical research.


Trevor, J. (2011) Can pay be strategic? A critical exploration of strategic pay in practice. London: Palgrave Macmillan.

Six principles of effective global talent management


One of the biggest challenges facing companies all over the world is building and sustaining a strong talent pipeline. Not only do businesses need to adjust to shifting demographics and work force preferences, but they must also build new capabilities and revitalize their organizations — all while investing in new technologies, globalizing their operations and contending with new competitors. What do companies operating in numerous markets need to do to attract and develop the very best employees so they can be competitive globally? To learn how leading multinational companies are facing up to the talent test, we examined both qualitative and quantitative data at leading companies from a wide range of industries all over the world.


Stahl, G.K., Björkman, I., Farndale, E., Morris, S.S., Paauwe, J., Stiles, P., Trevor, J. and Wright, P. (2012) "Six principles of effective global talent management." MIT Sloan Management Review, 53(2): 25-32



Human Capital Risk


Dr Jonathan Trevor

Cambridge Judge Business School

Not since the winter of discontent in 1979 has our economic and social well-being, societally and personally, been so dependent on how effectively people, as human capital, are managed. Ineffective HRM can inhibit organisational performance, destroy value and competitiveness; Dr Jonathan Trevor explains how the current financial crisis is an example of just such a human capital crisis
