Business Transformation Design and Delivery



ConsultCambridge has a proven track record of partnering with clients to enable the design and delivery of lasting change. Transformational change has allowed our clients to acquire new capabilities, improve organisational effectiveness and to align the organisation to new strategic priorities for long-term sustained success.

Our proprietary frameworks allow us to structure transformation for maximum impact for all stakeholders, aligning organisational change to strategic priorities and engaging the organisation through a compelling change narrative. We identify clearly the best approach to transformation, process design, operational implementation and robust measures.


The focus of our integrated and customized services encompass:




  • Transformation analysis, design and support: we help clients analyse the current state organisation relative to its potential future state and develop detailed planning and processes for the entire change life cycle to ensure lasting success.

  • Coaching top teams to articulate the transformation vision and inspire change: we actively engage and coach senior executives, as sponsors of transformation, to instil the capability necessary to articulate a compelling vision of the future organisation and inspire pro-active change support throughout the organisation.

  • Enabling transformation team capability, organisation and impact: we work closely with transformation teams to develop their role, constituency, capabilities and measures, to ensure effective transformation leadership.

  • Developing leaders as ambassadors of the future state organisation: we deliver in-house executive education tailored to develop leadership capability in support of transformation. The result is bespoke change capability and a measurable difference to the behaviours and values of key leaders throughout the organisation.

  • Building engagement and consensus throughout the organisation: we engage further with stakeholders throughout the wider organisation to overcome change resistance and generate momentum through implementation. We enable the emergence of collaborative leadership through targeted facilitation and education, empowering talent to exhibit their discretionary effort and take ownership of change.

Selected Insights:



Leadership Fit for the Information Age


Dr Jonathan Trevor and Professor Martin Kilduff


Organizations need to let go of previously held notions of leadership in order to embrace a new leadership concept and strategy more suited to today's environment. Future organizations will necessarily rely upon knowledge-intensive networks of highly connected and autonomous talent, empowered to rapidly converge on singular intersections of common interest without guidance from above. Such co-ordination will not be achieved through centralized command and control, but through network leadership - in effect, self-direction in the interests of a common purpose and guided by shared values.


Trevor, J. and Kilduff, M. (2012) "Leadership fit for the information age." Strategic HR Review, 11(3): 150-155



For Leadership read Fellowship


Dr Jonathan Trevor and Kate Tojeiro

Cambridge Judge Business School


Social media needs to be seen in the context of what we're seeing as the emergence of distributed leadership, where organisations are becoming flatter, leaner, more empowered, more democratic and more knowledge intensive. According to Kate Tojeiro and Dr Jonathan Trevor, people are no longer waiting for permission from on high to engage with each other and to make something new and exciting. We're seeing a leadership change being driven by social media.
